Green-person motion brief

Make the winning static move — not a new TV commercial. · 6–10 seconds · test 6s first

Sequence

  1. 0.0–0.5s — Full static composition already on screen
  2. 0.2–1.0s — Subtle person movement, crop push, or light parallax
  3. 0.3–1.2s — Headline resolves immediately (no long type animation)
  4. 1.0–3.0s — Benefit cards enter one at a time
  5. 3.0–5.5s — Complete offer remains readable
  6. Final 1–2s — Price / CTA subtle emphasis · loop cleanly

Layers that may move independently

personheadlinebenefit cardsoffer badgeCTAbackgroundlogo

Do

  • Slight push-in
  • Controlled parallax
  • Headline reveal
  • Benefit-card stagger
  • Badge emphasis
  • Soft background movement
  • Small glow changes

Avoid

  • Logo-only openings
  • Slow cinematic setup
  • Waiting several seconds for the main idea
  • Animating everything just because it can move

Sound, voice & Meta specs

The clips above are silent by design (Meta plays muted first — always caption-first). Toggle Voiceover to hear a rough browser text-to-speech read; that's a preview only. Real options below.

Human-quality voiceover

  • ElevenLabs — most natural; English + Spanish, cloned or stock voices.
  • OpenAI TTS (gpt-4o-mini-tts) — fast, cheap, good for scratch VO.
  • Azure / Google Neural — enterprise, many Spanish locales.
  • Browser TTS (used here) — free, instant, robotic — preview only.

Music & SFX (licensed)

  • Meta Sound Collection — free, cleared for Meta ads.
  • Artlist / Epidemic Sound — subscription, broad library.
  • Keep beds subtle; design so the ad works with sound OFF.
  • Add whoosh/tick SFX on each transition to sell the motion.

Meta video specs (2026)

  • Feed 4:5 · 1080×1350 · 15–30s sweet spot.
  • Reels / Stories 9:16 · 1080×1920 · 6–15s.
  • Square 1:1 · 1080×1080 (Marketplace / carousel).
  • Hook in the first 2s. H.264 MP4, ≤4GB, 30fps.

9:16 safe zone

  • Keep text/logos out of the top ~250px (handle/close).
  • And the bottom ~340–670px (caption + CTA button).
  • Design key content in the center band.
  • One 9:16 master covers both Reels and Stories.

Production Paths

Animate the approved hierarchy: hook → role clarity → benefits → offer/CTA. Prefer 6s / 10s / 15s cuts from each master after static QA.

  • No pink — ColorPlate enforces the approved palette.
  • No unapproved price/HIPAA badge baked in.
  • Captions on every spot.
  • MedVirtual logo only (never MedVirtual.ai).

npm run remotion:studio · src/remotion/index.js

  • Open Remotion Studio and pick a VMA composition.
  • Set the colorFamilyId prop to an approved family — the ColorPlate rejects non-approved hexes.
  • Confirm headline + benefits come from approved copy (vma-copy-en / vma-copy-es).
  • Keep PriceTicket / TrustSeal hidden unless the linked claim is Approved for Launch.
  • Preview all three aspect ratios; check 9:16 safe zones.
  • Render to .local-masters/renders — never commit large MP4s to public.

<ColorPlate />

Full-frame background plate driven by a color-family id. Enforces approved hexes only.

colorFamilyId

<KineticHeadline />

Scale/wipe-in billboard headline with mobile-safe type scale.

text, colorFamilyId, delay

<BenefitList />

Staggered benefit bullets with animated check icons (max 4).

items[], colorFamilyId

<AbstractPerson />

CSS/SVG abstract admin figure reveal — no photo, no pink.

colorFamilyId, side

<TrustSeal />

Dedicated-staff / approved-claim seal pop. Hidden unless claim approved.

claimId, approved

<PriceTicket />

Angled price ticket — renders only when price claim is approved.

text, approved

<CTAButton />

Pulsing CTA pill.

label, colorFamilyId

<LogoLockup />

MedVirtual white logo fade-in (never MedVirtual.ai).

delay

<CaptionTrack />

Burned-in captions for sound-off viewing.

lines[]

visible
