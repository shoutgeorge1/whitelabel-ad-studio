# Competitor Ad Notes — Strategy Only

**Do not implement in creative yet.** Documented for the next layout/copy pass.

## Pattern 1: Text on image is common
Competitors put strong text directly on the creative — large, clear hooks. Not tiny paragraphs.

**MedVirtual implication:** Text on images, one big idea per creative, mobile-readable.

## Pattern 2: Short-form vertical matters
Many ads built for mobile-first video/shorts placements.

**MedVirtual implication:** 9:16 crops must work. Static images should be reel-ready. First frame = standalone hook.

## Pattern 3: Face-forward creative
Faces, talking heads, testimonial-style framing common.

**MedVirtual implication:** Keep faces, but prioritize **headset + laptop work context** over plain headshots for first batch.

## Pattern 4: Native beats overly polished
Competitors look simple and feed-native, not corporate commercial.

**MedVirtual implication:** Avoid SaaS landing-page polish. Direct, readable, mobile-first.

## Pattern 5: Direct hooks
Problems stated fast — missed calls, overloaded front desk, hiring gaps.

**Future hook angles (not built yet):**
- Missed calls
- Overloaded front desk
- Scheduling bottlenecks
- Hiring gaps
- Admin backlog
- Follow-up gaps

## Pattern 6: Testimonial / POV style likely matters
Build short-form track later. Do not fake testimonials. Use practice-operations POV instead.

**Future styles (not built yet):**
- "When your front desk is buried…"
- "When patient calls keep coming in…"
- "When hiring takes too long…"
- "Three tasks your medical office can delegate…"
